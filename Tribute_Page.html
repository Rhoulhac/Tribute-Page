Dr. Albert Mohler

President of Southern Baptist Theological Seminary

Dr. Mohler preaching to his congregation

Here's a time line of Dr. Mohler's life:

  • 1959 - Born October 19 in Lakeland, FL
  • 1983 - Joined staff at Southern Baptist Theological Seminary in Louisville, Kentucky as Coordinator of Foundation Support
  • 1985 - Became Associate Editor of bi-monthly Preaching Magazine
  • 1987 - Became Director of Capital Funding
  • 1993 - Appointed the 9th President of the Southern Baptist Theological Seminary
  • 2001 - Served on the Advisory Council for the English Standard Version (ESV) of the Bible
  • 2002 - Appeared on MSNBC's Donahue speaking on the topic of Christian evangelization of Jews
  • 2003 - Interviewed by the Time on the subject of evangelizing Iraqi Muslims
  • 2004 - Debated retired Eposcopal bishop John Shelby Spong on Faith Under Fire on the topic of the historicity and truthfulness of the Bible
  • 2004 - Spoke at the annual meeting of the Florida Baptist State Convention
  • 2005 - Gave Commencement address at Union University in Jackson, Tennessee
  • 2008 - Declined to sign An Evangelical Manifesto, publishing a lengthly explanation for his decision
  • 2013 - Appeared on CNN to discuss the controversy surrounding comments made by Phil Robertson of Duck Dynasty
  • 2014 - Delivered a Forum Lecture at Brigham Young University titled, "Strengthen the Things that Remain: Human Dignity, Human Rights, and Human Flourishing in a Dangerous Age."
